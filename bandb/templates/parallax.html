A night under the Moon and stars!

One clear April night in 2014, we both found ourselves at an open observatory night at the National Air & Space Museum. Katelyn, a mutual friend Billy knew from his time at JPL and Betsy knew from the Maker project at APL, had invited us each to meet her a and few friends there.

We both got there early, so got in line to look through the big telescope. While we waited, we discovered we have a lot in common, and by the time we got the the front, we barely thought about the telesccope. It was pointed at the moon, and when we asked what we were looking at, the girl told us, "It's the moon!"

At the end of the night, Billy gave Betsy a ride home, and asked her to dinner. We met up again a week later at Jaleo for our first date, and were there for three hours! Afterwards, we got gelato and took a walk under the moonlight accross the National Mall.

Ever since those early nights, the Moon has been special to us! So under the full moon on a chilly February night, standing on the National Mall near the Air & Space Museum, we got engaged, and then went for a beautiful hike under the moonlight through the National Arboretum.

Now we're looking forward to celebrating our wedding with everybody, again, under the light of the full moon!
